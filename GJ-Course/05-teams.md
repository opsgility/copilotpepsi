# Exercise 5: Teams — Meeting Recaps & Action Items (45 min)
---

Copilot in Teams helps you stay on top of meetings and conversations — even the ones you missed. Key capabilities:

- **Meeting recaps** — After a meeting with transcription enabled, Copilot summarizes key discussion points, decisions, and action items.
- **Action item extraction** — Ask Copilot to list who committed to what, with deadlines.
- **Catch up on missed meetings** — Review what happened without watching a recording.
- **Chat summaries** — Summarize long chat threads to get up to speed quickly.

**How it works:** Copilot uses the meeting transcript to answer your questions. Each participant's prompts are private — only you see your Copilot responses.

---

### Task 1: Summarize a leadership meeting (15 min)

You missed a cross-functional leadership meeting and need to catch up quickly.

1. In **Teams for the web**, select the **Copilot** icon in the left navigation pane.
2. Ensure the **Work** toggle is selected.
3. Attach the **GJ_Leadership_Meeting_Transcript.docx** file from your OneDrive.
4. Enter:

    **Analyze this meeting transcript from a G&J Pepsi leadership meeting. Provide: (1) A 3-4 sentence summary of the overall meeting, (2) Key decisions that were made, (3) Action items with owners and deadlines, (4) Any unresolved issues or disagreements. Format with clear headings.**

5. Review the summary. Ask a follow-up focused on your department:

    **What was discussed about [your department — e.g., HR, IT, Sales, Operations]? Were any action items assigned to that team?**

6. Try a different angle:

    **What were the top 3 strategic priorities discussed in this meeting? For each, what was the group's conclusion?**

---

### Task 2: Extract action items from a supply chain review (15 min)

The supply chain team held a quarterly review. You need a structured summary of commitments made.

1. In **Teams Copilot**, attach the **GJ_Supply_Chain_Review_Transcript.docx** file.
2. Enter:

    **Extract all action items from this supply chain review meeting. For each action item, include: (1) What needs to be done, (2) Who is responsible, (3) The deadline or timeframe mentioned, (4) Any dependencies or blockers noted. Format as a numbered list.**

3. Review the action items. Ask Copilot to categorize them:

    **Group these action items by priority: Critical (must complete this quarter), Important (should complete this quarter), and Nice-to-have (can defer if needed). Explain your reasoning for each categorization.**

4. Now ask Copilot to create a follow-up communication:

    **Draft a brief follow-up email summarizing these action items that I could send to all meeting participants. Include a table with columns for Action Item, Owner, Deadline, and Status (all set to "Not Started"). Keep it professional and concise.**

5. Copy the email draft — you could paste this into Outlook to actually send it.

### Task 3: Catch up and summarize conversations (15 min)

**Part A: Summarize a meeting for someone who wasn't there**

1. Using the leadership meeting transcript, ask Copilot:

    **Write a 5-bullet summary of this meeting that I can send to a colleague who wasn't present. Focus on decisions and next steps, not discussion details. Keep it under 200 words.**

2. Compare this summary to the detailed one from Task 1. Notice how prompting for a different format and audience changes the output.

**Part B: Practice with live Teams features**

If you have recent Teams meetings with transcription enabled:

1. Open a recent meeting in your **Teams Calendar**.
2. Select the **Recap** tab (or look for Copilot in the meeting details).
3. Try these prompts:
    - "What were the main topics discussed?"
    - "List all action items mentioned in this meeting"
    - "What questions were raised but not answered?"
    - "Summarize what [person's name] said about [topic]"

> [!TIP]
> **For live meetings:** When you're in a Teams meeting with transcription on, you can open Copilot during the meeting and ask questions in real-time: "What have I missed so far?", "Summarize the discussion about the budget", or "What action items have been mentioned?"

**Part C: Chat summaries**

1. Open any Teams chat thread that has 10+ messages.
2. Select the **Copilot** icon at the top of the chat.
3. Ask: "Summarize this chat. What were the key decisions and open questions?"

> [!NOTE]
> Copilot in Teams chat can summarize up to 30 days of content within a single chat thread. It only accesses the thread you're viewing — it cannot reference other chats or channels.

### Task 4: Channel and chat summaries (bonus, 10 min)

Copilot in Teams can summarize not just meetings, but also channel conversations and group chats — helping you catch up on discussions you missed.

**Part A: Channel summaries**

1. Open a **Teams channel** that has had recent activity (at least 5–10 posts).

2. Select the **Copilot** icon at the top of the channel.

3. Enter:

    **Summarize the key discussions in this channel from the past week. What decisions were made? What questions are still open? What topics got the most engagement?**

4. Follow up with a targeted question:

    **Were there any requests or action items directed at [your department]?**

**Part B: Group chat summaries**

1. Open a **group chat** in Teams that has 15+ messages.

2. Select the **Copilot** icon at the top of the chat.

3. Enter:

    **Summarize this chat thread. What was the main topic? Were any decisions made or next steps agreed on?**

4. Try a more targeted prompt:

    **What questions were asked in this chat that haven't been answered yet?**

**Part C: Real-time meeting assistance**

If you're in a **live Teams meeting** with transcription enabled:

1. Open the **Copilot** pane during the meeting.
2. Try these real-time prompts:
    - "What have I missed so far?"
    - "Summarize the discussion about [topic]"
    - "What action items have been mentioned so far?"
    - "What questions have been raised that haven't been answered?"

> [!NOTE]
> Copilot in Teams channels can summarize up to 30 days of content. It only accesses channels and chats you already have access to — it follows the same permissions model as the rest of Microsoft 365.
