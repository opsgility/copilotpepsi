# Wrap-Up & Q&A (30 min)
---

> **PPTX Reference:** This section corresponds to **Slides 43–47** of the GJ_Pepsi_Copilot_Workshop.pptx deck.

### Key Takeaways

| Takeaway | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Prompt quality is everything** | Specific context produces useful output. Generic prompts produce generic results. Always use Goal, Context, Sources, Expectations. |
| **Copilot is your draft partner** | Always review, iterate, and personalize. Copilot gets you 80% there — your expertise closes the gap. |
| **Every app has a sweet spot** | Outlook: email drafting and coaching. Teams: meeting recaps. Excel: data analysis. Word: document creation. PowerPoint: presentations. Agents: repeatable Q&A. |
| **Start small, build momentum** | Pick one workflow this week where Copilot can save you time. Master it. Then expand to the next one. |

### Copilot Quick Reference — What to Use When

| I need to... | Use... |
|-------------|--------|
| Research a topic or brainstorm ideas | **Copilot Chat** (Web mode) |
| Summarize internal documents or find info across my org | **Copilot Chat** (Work mode) |
| Draft or rewrite a document | **Copilot in Word** |
| Analyze data, create charts, or add formulas | **Copilot in Excel** |
| Draft an email, summarize a thread, or check tone | **Copilot in Outlook** |
| Catch up on a meeting or extract action items | **Copilot in Teams** |
| Create a presentation from scratch or from a document | **Copilot in PowerPoint** |
| Build a reusable Q&A bot for your team | **Copilot Studio Agents** |
