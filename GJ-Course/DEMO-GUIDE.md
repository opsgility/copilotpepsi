# Demo Guide — G&J Pepsi Copilot Workshop

For each demo below, the trainer performs the demo live while narrating. Demos are 3-5 minutes each, designed to show the key capability before participants try it in the exercise.

---

## Demo 1: Copilot Chat — Web vs Work Mode
**When:** After Copilot Fundamentals, before Exercise 1
**Duration:** 4 minutes

### Setup
- Have microsoft365.com open in Edge, signed in

### Steps
1. Open Copilot Chat from the left nav
2. Show the Web/Work toggle at the top
3. In **Web mode**, type: "What are the top 3 sustainability trends in beverage distribution for 2026?"
4. Show how Copilot returns internet-grounded results with citations
5. Switch to **Work mode**
6. Attach the GJ_Quarterly_Business_Review.docx file
7. Type: "What were the top 3 risks identified in this quarterly business review?"
8. Show how Work mode pulls directly from the uploaded document

### Talking Points
- Web mode = external research, industry trends, general knowledge
- Work mode = your organization's files, emails, meetings, chats via Microsoft Graph
- You can switch between modes in the same conversation
- Copilot only accesses data you already have permission to see
- Think of Web mode as your research analyst, Work mode as your internal knowledge base

## Demo 2: Copilot in Word — Draft & Summarize
**When:** Before Exercise 2 (Word)
**Duration:** 5 minutes

### Setup
- Have Word for the web open with a blank document
- Have GJ_Route_Optimization_Proposal.docx uploaded to OneDrive

### Steps
1. Show the Draft with Copilot prompt field that appears above the blank document
2. Attach the GJ_Route_Optimization_Proposal.docx file
3. Type: "Summarize this route optimization proposal into a one-page executive summary for senior leadership. Include the problem, solution, expected ROI, and timeline. Use clear headings."
4. Show draft 1 — point out the structure and how it pulled real data
5. Refine: "Add the specific dollar amounts for projected savings and investment cost. Make the ROI more prominent."
6. Show draft 2 — use arrows to compare
7. Click "Keep it" to insert
8. Click into the document and type something to exit draft mode
9. Open the Copilot pane (icon in menu bar)
10. In the pane, type: "What are the biggest risks in this proposal?"

### Talking Points
- Draft mode: Copilot writes directly into the document, you navigate numbered drafts
- Once you type, draft mode ends — switch to the Copilot pane for follow-up questions
- Attaching files gives Copilot real data instead of generic placeholder content
- In the exercise, you'll draft communications, summarize proposals, and rewrite for different audiences
- This works the same whether you're in HR drafting a policy or IT writing a change notification

## Demo 3: Copilot in Excel — Data Analysis & Charts
**When:** Before Exercise 3 (Excel)
**Duration:** 5 minutes

### Setup
- Have GJ_Q4_Sales_Performance.xlsx open in Excel for the web
- Ensure data is formatted as a Table and AutoSave is on

### Steps
1. Click Copilot in the Excel ribbon
2. Type: "Summarize the key trends in this Q4 sales data. Which regions exceeded their targets and which fell short?"
3. Show the analysis response
4. Type: "Create a bar chart showing Q4 total revenue by region, sorted highest to lowest"
5. Insert the chart into a new sheet
6. Type: "Which product categories are growing fastest? Show me the variance vs target for each category."
7. Show the category analysis
8. Type: "Add a column called 'Performance Rating' that labels each row as 'Exceeds' if variance is above 5%, 'Meets' if between -5% and 5%, and 'Below' if under -5%."
9. Show the formula column insertion

### Talking Points
- Data must be in a Table for Copilot to work (Insert > Table)
- Copilot can analyze, visualize, AND add formula columns to your data
- It works directly in your workbook — no need to export or copy-paste
- In the exercise, you'll analyze both sales data and warehouse operations data
- Always verify Copilot's calculations — it's a powerful starting point, not the final answer

## Demo 4: Copilot in Outlook — Draft, Summarize & Coach
**When:** Before Exercise 4 (Outlook)
**Duration:** 4 minutes

### Steps
1. Create a new email in Outlook on the web
2. Click the Open Copilot (pencil) icon
3. Type: "Draft an email to all department heads announcing a company-wide sustainability initiative. We need one representative from each department for a bi-weekly task force starting May 1st. Explain why this matters to our business. Professional but energizing tone."
4. Show draft 1 — point out the structure
5. Refine: "Add 3 bullet points about what the task force will accomplish in its first quarter."
6. Show draft 2 — compare with arrows
7. Click "Keep it"
8. Now demonstrate coaching: type a rough 2-sentence email manually
9. Highlight it, click the pencil icon, select "Coaching by Copilot"
10. Show the tone and clarity feedback
11. Quickly show "Change Tone" — toggle between Formal and Direct

### Talking Points
- Draft: Copilot writes the first version, you iterate until it's right
- Each refinement creates a new numbered draft you can compare
- Coaching: get feedback on tone, clarity, and how the reader will perceive it
- Especially valuable for sensitive communications — performance conversations, cross-functional announcements
- In the exercise, you'll draft for different audiences and practice coaching on your own emails

## Demo 5: Copilot in Teams — Meeting Recap & Action Items
**When:** Before Exercise 5 (Teams)
**Duration:** 4 minutes

### Setup
- Have Teams open with Copilot in the left nav
- Have GJ_Leadership_Meeting_Transcript.docx ready to attach

### Steps
1. Open Copilot in Teams, ensure Work toggle is selected
2. Attach the leadership meeting transcript
3. Type: "Summarize this leadership meeting. What were the key decisions made and action items assigned?"
4. Show the structured summary with decisions and owners
5. Type: "What was discussed about the IT infrastructure upgrade? What was the main concern?"
6. Show how Copilot filters to a specific topic
7. Type: "Create a list of all action items with who's responsible and the deadline. Format as a table."
8. Show the table format — point out this is ready to paste into an email or project tracker

### Talking Points
- Copilot uses the transcript to answer any question about the meeting
- You can filter by topic, by person, or by type of discussion (decisions, risks, action items)
- In live meetings with transcription on, you can use Copilot in real-time
- The table output is designed to be copied into emails, task trackers, or project plans
- In the exercise, you'll work with both a leadership meeting and a supply chain review

## Demo 6: Copilot in PowerPoint — Presentation from a Document
**When:** Before Exercise 6 (PowerPoint)
**Duration:** 5 minutes

### Setup
- Have PowerPoint for the web open, blank presentation
- Have GJ_Quarterly_Business_Review.docx in OneDrive

### Steps
1. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon
2. Select "Create a presentation from file..."
3. Attach GJ_Quarterly_Business_Review.docx
4. Type: "Create a 10-slide presentation from this quarterly business review for an all-hands meeting. Focus on Q4 highlights, revenue performance, operational updates, and next quarter priorities."
5. Show the generated deck — flip through 3-4 slides
6. Open the Copilot pane and type: "Add speaker notes to the first slide with 3 key talking points and a transition to slide 2."
7. Show the speaker notes
8. Type: "The operational excellence slide is too text-heavy. Simplify to 5 short bullet points."
9. Show the refined slide

### Talking Points
- Copilot transforms documents into presentations — no more manual slide building
- It respects the document structure (headings become slide titles)
- You can also create from a prompt without any source document
- After creation, refine individual slides — add notes, simplify content, request new slides
- In the exercise, you'll create both from prompts and from documents, plus department-specific decks

## Demo 7: Copilot Studio — Building an Employee FAQ Agent
**When:** Before Exercise 7 (Agents)
**Duration:** 5 minutes

### Setup
- Have microsoft365.com open

### Steps
1. Click "New agent" in the left navigation pane
2. In the prompt, type: "Create an agent called 'G&J Benefits Assistant' that helps employees answer questions about company benefits. It should only use uploaded documents as knowledge sources and respond in a friendly, professional tone."
3. Show the Agent Builder creating the agent (Describe tab)
4. Switch to the Configure tab — show the auto-generated Name, Description, and Instructions
5. Scroll to Knowledge — show the upload area
6. Scroll to Suggested Prompts — show how they're auto-generated
7. DON'T actually create it (participants will build their own) — just show the interface

### Talking Points
- Agents are custom AI assistants that answer questions from YOUR documents
- No programming required — describe what you want in natural language
- Agent Builder auto-generates instructions based on your description
- Knowledge sources: upload up to 20 files (docx, xlsx, pdf, pptx)
- Agents are private by default — share with specific people or your org
- In the exercise, you'll build a full Employee FAQ agent and a department-specific agent

## General Demo Tips

- **Narrate as you go** — explain what you're typing and why before hitting Enter
- **Show the iteration** — always refine at least once to demonstrate that Copilot is a draft partner
- **Acknowledge imperfection** — if Copilot gives a mediocre response, use it as a teaching moment about prompt quality
- **Connect to their world** — after each demo, briefly note how this applies across departments (Accounting, HR, IT, Sales, Ops, Supply Chain)
- **Keep it to 3-5 minutes** — demos are appetizers, not the main course. Exercises are where learning happens.
- **Use their data** — always demo with the G&J resource files, not generic examples. This makes it immediately relatable.
